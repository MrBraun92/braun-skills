# Video Performance Diagnostics Framework

## Diagnostic Metrics Hierarchy

### Primary Retention Metrics
- **Hook hold (0-2s)**: Critical window for pattern interrupts and curiosity triggers
- **Early retention (0-5s)**: Validates hook effectiveness and initial value proposition
- **Mid retention (0-10s)**: Tests content delivery and pacing
- **Completion rate**: Overall watch-through percentage
- **Rewatch signals**: Indicates high-value content or loop optimization

### Engagement Intent Signals
- **Save rate**: High-intent signal indicating future reference value
- **Share rate**: Indicates social currency and viral potential
- **Comment quality**: Engagement depth and community building
- **Profile visits**: Interest in creator beyond single video

### Distribution Indicators
- **Distribution velocity**: Speed of initial push (first 1-3 hours)
- **Delayed push patterns**: Secondary waves indicating category re-learning
- **Audience segmentation**: Language, region, and behavioral splits
- **Throttling vs suppression**: Distinguishing between slow distribution and algorithmic penalties

## Performance Diagnosis Decision Tree

### Video Stalled (Low Views)
1. **Check hook hold (0-2s)**
   - Below 50%: Hook failure → Redesign opening frame/audio
   - Above 50%: Move to retention analysis

2. **Analyze retention slope**
   - Sharp drop at specific timestamp: Content pacing issue at that point
   - Gradual decline: Value delivery problem throughout
   - Flat then drop: Payoff timing misaligned with viewer patience

3. **Review category signals**
   - Mixed niche indicators: Algorithmic confusion → Clarify content pillar
   - Clear category but low distribution: Trust signal deficit or audience saturation

### High Views, Low Engagement
1. **Passive watch vs active engagement gap**
   - High completion, low saves/shares: Entertainment without utility
   - High completion, low comments: Missing conversation trigger
   - Solution: Add actionable takeaways or discussion prompts

2. **Audience mismatch**
   - Wrong demographic reached: Caption/audio/visual signals misaligned
   - Solution: Refine keyword targeting and visual cues

### Inconsistent Performance
1. **Category learning cycles**
   - Algorithm testing content fit across niches
   - Solution: Maintain consistent content pillars for 7-10 videos

2. **Posting cadence effects**
   - Irregular posting confuses exploration pool assignment
   - Solution: Establish predictable rhythm (3-5x/week minimum)

## Retention Engineering Patterns

### Hook Formulas by Content Type

**Educational/Tutorial**
- Pattern interrupt (unexpected visual) + Problem statement + Quick payoff preview
- Example: "Stop doing [common mistake]" → Show correct method → "Here's why"

**Storytime/Narrative**
- Curiosity gap + Emotional stake + Timeline anchor
- Example: "I almost lost everything because..." → Stakes established → "It started when..."

**Entertainment/Comedy**
- Contrast setup + Immediate punchline + Extended payoff
- Example: Visual gag in first frame → Audio punchline → Callback loop

**Product/Review**
- Bold claim + Visual proof + Credibility signal
- Example: "This changed my [routine]" → Product in use → "After 30 days..."

### Retention Optimization Techniques
- **Micro-open loops**: Pose question at 3s, answer at 8s, create new loop
- **Visual pacing**: Cut every 1.5-2 seconds to maintain attention
- **Audio layering**: Voiceover + music + sound effects for depth
- **On-screen text**: Reinforce spoken keywords for search indexing and accessibility
- **Loop optimization**: Seamless end-to-start transition for rewatch probability

## Trust Signal vs Spam Signal Matrix

### Trust Signals
- Consistent posting cadence (3-5x/week)
- Original audio or properly credited sounds
- Natural caption language (not keyword stuffing)
- Authentic engagement in comments
- Profile completion (bio, profile pic, linked accounts)
- Gradual follower growth aligned with view patterns

### Spam Signals
- Irregular posting (gaps then bursts)
- Excessive hashtags (>5) or irrelevant tags
- Caption keyword stuffing
- Engagement bait ("comment for part 2")
- Follow/unfollow patterns
- Sudden follower spikes without view correlation
- External link spam in bio or comments
