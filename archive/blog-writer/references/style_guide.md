# Blog Writing Style Guide

## Tone and Voice

**Conversational yet professional** - Write as if explaining to a knowledgeable colleague over coffee. Avoid overly formal language while maintaining credibility.

**Active voice preferred** - "We analyzed the data" instead of "The data was analyzed."

**Personal pronouns encouraged** - Use "you" to address readers directly, "we" for collaborative tone.

## Structure

### Opening Hook (First 2-3 sentences)
- Start with a compelling question, statistic, or relatable scenario
- Immediately establish relevance to the reader
- Avoid generic introductions like "In today's world..."

### Body Sections
- Use descriptive H2 headings (not "Introduction" or "Section 1")
- Keep paragraphs to 3-4 sentences maximum
- Include subheadings (H3) every 200-300 words
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Include examples or case studies to illustrate points

### Conclusion
- Summarize key takeaways (3-5 bullet points)
- End with a clear call-to-action or thought-provoking question
- Avoid introducing new information

## Formatting

**Bold** for emphasis on key terms (first mention only)

*Italics* for subtle emphasis or foreign terms

`Code formatting` for technical terms, commands, or file names

> Blockquotes for important callouts or expert quotes

## Length Guidelines

- **Short-form**: 600-800 words (quick tips, news commentary)
- **Standard**: 1,200-1,500 words (how-to guides, explainers)
- **Long-form**: 2,000-3,000 words (comprehensive guides, research-backed articles)

## SEO Best Practices

- Include target keyword in H1, first paragraph, and one H2
- Use semantic variations of keywords naturally
- Write meta description (150-160 characters)
- Include 2-3 internal links to related content
- Add alt text for all images

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Keyword stuffing** - Write for humans first, search engines second
- **Wall of text** - Break up long paragraphs with headings and whitespace
- **Jargon overload** - Define technical terms on first use
- **Clickbait headlines** - Be compelling but honest
- **Passive voice** - "Mistakes were made" → "We made mistakes"
